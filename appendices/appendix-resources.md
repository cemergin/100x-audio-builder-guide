# Appendix C: Recommended Reading, Channels, and Communities

> An annotated guide to books, YouTube channels, forums, online resources, software, courses, and kit suppliers for continuing your audio building education. Not a link dump — every entry is here because it's genuinely useful, and the annotation tells you why.

---

## 1. Books

### Electronics Fundamentals

**Practical Electronics for Inventors** — Paul Scherz & Simon Monk
- **Level:** Beginner to Intermediate
- **Why it's essential:** The single best reference book for self-taught electronics builders. Covers everything from basic theory through op-amps, power supplies, digital logic, and microcontrollers. Clear explanations, excellent diagrams, and practical focus. If you buy one electronics book, make it this one.
- **Best chapters for audio builders:** Op-amp circuits, filters, power supply design, transistor amplifiers.
- *(Complements: Part 0 — Electronics Fundamentals)*

**The Art of Electronics** — Paul Horowitz & Winfield Hill
- **Level:** Intermediate to Advanced
- **Why it's essential:** The definitive electronics textbook. Deeper and more rigorous than Scherz, with emphasis on practical design intuition over pure theory. The companion *Learning the Art of Electronics* (lab manual) is excellent for hands-on learning.
- **Best chapters for audio builders:** Transistor circuit design, feedback theory, low-noise techniques, power supply regulation.
- **Note:** Expensive ($100+) but worth every penny. You'll reference it for years.
- *(Complements: Part 0, Part 3)*

**Make: Electronics** — Charles Platt
- **Level:** Absolute Beginner
- **Why it's essential:** The gentlest introduction to electronics. Hands-on from page one — you're building circuits in the first chapter. Best for readers with zero electronics background who want to learn by doing.
- *(Complements: Part 0)*

### Speaker Design and Construction

**Loudspeaker Design Cookbook** — Vance Dickason
- **Level:** Intermediate to Advanced
- **Why it's essential:** THE reference for speaker enclosure design. Covers sealed, ported, bandpass, transmission line, and horn-loaded designs with thorough mathematical treatment. Includes extensive tables of Thiele-Small parameters for commercial drivers.
- **Best chapters for audio builders:** Sealed and ported enclosure design, crossover network theory, driver selection methodology.
- **Note:** Dense and technical. Not a beginner's first read, but indispensable once you're designing your own speakers.
- *(Complements: Part 2 — Speakers)*

**Testing Loudspeakers** — Joe D'Appolito
- **Level:** Intermediate to Advanced
- **Why it's essential:** The definitive guide to measuring speaker performance. Covers driver testing, crossover measurement, system measurement, and room acoustics with practical procedures.
- *(Complements: Part 2)*

**Speaker Building 201** — Ray Alden
- **Level:** Beginner to Intermediate
- **Why it's essential:** A gentler introduction to speaker building than Dickason. Project-oriented with complete build plans. Good stepping stone before tackling the Design Cookbook.
- *(Complements: Part 2)*

**High Performance Loudspeakers** — Martin Colloms
- **Level:** Advanced
- **Why it's essential:** Academic treatment of loudspeaker design covering advanced topics like diffraction modeling, cone breakup analysis, and subjective evaluation methodology.
- *(Complements: Part 2)*

### Amplifier Design

**Design and Construction of Tube Guitar Amplifiers** — Robert Megantz
- **Level:** Beginner to Intermediate
- **Why it's essential:** The most accessible book on tube guitar amp design and construction. Walks through each section of a guitar amplifier (preamp, tone stack, phase inverter, power amp, power supply) with clear explanations. Includes complete build projects.
- *(Complements: Part 3 — Amplifiers)*

**Designing Tube Preamps for Guitar and Bass** — Merlin Blencowe
- **Level:** Intermediate to Advanced
- **Why it's essential:** Deep dive into tube preamp circuit design. Goes far beyond cookbook designs — teaches you to calculate gain, frequency response, and distortion characteristics from first principles. If you want to design original preamp circuits (not just build clones), this is the book.
- *(Complements: Part 3)*

**Designing High-Fidelity Tube Preamps** — Merlin Blencowe
- **Level:** Intermediate to Advanced
- **Why it's essential:** Blencowe's companion book focused on hi-fi (rather than guitar) preamp design. Covers RIAA phono stages, line stages, and headphone amplifiers with emphasis on low distortion and low noise.
- *(Complements: Part 3)*

**The Radiotron Designer's Handbook** — F. Langford-Smith (ed.)
- **Level:** Advanced
- **Why it's essential:** The original comprehensive reference for vacuum tube circuit design (1953). Over 1,500 pages covering every aspect of tube electronics. Available as a reprint or free PDF from TubeBooks.org. Dense but authoritative.
- *(Complements: Part 3)*

**Audio Power Amplifier Design** — Douglas Self
- **Level:** Intermediate to Advanced
- **Why it's essential:** The definitive reference for solid-state power amplifier design. Covers Class A, AB, and D topologies with rigorous analysis of distortion mechanisms. Practical design methodology with real schematics.
- *(Complements: Part 3)*

**Small Signal Audio Design** — Douglas Self
- **Level:** Intermediate to Advanced
- **Why it's essential:** Companion to the power amp book, covering preamp, tone control, filter, and signal processing circuit design. Comprehensive treatment of noise, distortion, and practical circuit building blocks.
- *(Complements: Part 3)*

### Guitar Effects

**Electronic Projects for Musicians** — Craig Anderton
- **Level:** Beginner
- **Why it's essential:** A classic that introduced many people to effects building. Project-oriented with straightforward designs. Some circuits are dated but the educational approach is timeless.
- *(Complements: Part 1 — Guitar Pedals)*

**How to Modify Guitar Pedals** — Brian Wampler
- **Level:** Beginner to Intermediate
- **Why it's essential:** Teaches you how pedal circuits work by showing how to modify them. Once you understand what each component does and how changing it affects the sound, you can design your own circuits. Practical, hands-on approach.
- *(Complements: Part 1)*

### PCB Design

**KiCad Like a Pro** — Dr. Peter Dalmaris
- **Level:** Beginner to Intermediate
- **Why it's essential:** Step-by-step guide to PCB design using KiCad. Takes you from schematic entry through board layout to manufacturing files.
- *(Complements: Part 5)*

## 2. YouTube Channels

### Tube Amplifiers

**Uncle Doug**
- **Focus:** Tube amplifier theory and construction
- **Why watch:** Possibly the best educator on YouTube for tube amp electronics. Uncle Doug explains complex concepts (load lines, bias, phase inverters, negative feedback) with clarity and humor using hand-drawn diagrams. His multi-part series on how tube amps work is a masterclass.
- **Start with:** "How a Tube Amp Works" series
- *(Complements: Part 3)*

**Rob Robinette (video content)**
- **Focus:** Tube amp modification and repair
- **Why watch:** Complements his exceptional website (see Section 4). Clear, practical demonstrations of amp modifications.

### Guitar Pedals

**JHS Pedals**
- **Focus:** Pedal history, deep dives, comparisons
- **Why watch:** Josh Scott's passion for pedal history is infectious. The "History of..." series traces classic effects from their origins through modern iterations. The show helps you understand *why* circuits were designed the way they were — invaluable context for your own designs.
- **Start with:** "History of the Tube Screamer" or "History of the Fuzz Face"
- *(Complements: Part 1)*

**DIY Guitar Pedals**
- **Focus:** Building guitar pedals from scratch
- **Why watch:** Straightforward build videos. Good for seeing the actual construction process — component selection, soldering technique, enclosure work, testing.
- *(Complements: Part 1)*

**EEVblog**
- **Focus:** Electronics engineering, teardowns, tutorials
- **Why watch:** Dave Jones's no-nonsense electronics education. Not audio-specific, but his fundamentals videos (Ohm's Law, capacitors, oscilloscope usage) are the best on YouTube. Teardown videos teach you what good (and bad) electronics design looks like.
- **Start with:** "EEVblog #831 — Soldering Tutorial" and the multimeter tutorial series
- *(Complements: Part 0)*

**That Pedal Show**
- **Focus:** Guitar effects and tone
- **Why watch:** Dan and Mick demonstrate and compare effects in musical context. Useful for understanding what players want from effects — which informs your product design.
- *(Complements: Part 1)*

**Brian Wampler / Wampler Pedals**
- **Focus:** Pedal design, modification, and circuit analysis
- **Why watch:** Brian explains the "why" behind his circuit design choices. Useful for transitioning from building clones to designing original circuits.
- *(Complements: Part 1)*

### Speakers and Audio

**Toid's DIY Audio**
- **Focus:** DIY speaker building
- **Why watch:** One of the best channels dedicated specifically to DIY speaker construction. Build videos, measurements, comparisons, and practical advice.
- **Start with:** Any of his bookshelf speaker build series
- *(Complements: Part 2)*

**123Toid**
- **Focus:** DIY speaker building (companion to Toid's DIY Audio)
- **Why watch:** Additional build content and designs.
- *(Complements: Part 2)*

**Kirby Meets Audio**
- **Focus:** Audio measurements, speaker design, acoustic analysis
- **Why watch:** Data-driven approach to audio. Good for learning how to interpret measurements and correlate them with listening impressions.
- *(Complements: Part 2)*

**PS Audio (Paul McGowan)**
- **Focus:** Hi-fi audio concepts
- **Why watch:** Short daily videos explaining audio concepts (what is jitter? what does a DAC do? why do cables matter or not?). Good for building your vocabulary and understanding the audiophile perspective.
- *(Complements: Part 4)*

### General Electronics

**Ben Eater**
- **Focus:** Digital electronics from first principles
- **Why watch:** Builds a computer from scratch on breadboards. Exceptional educator. If you want to understand the digital side of audio (DACs, ADCs, microprocessors), start here.
- *(Complements: Part 4)*

**GreatScott!**
- **Focus:** Practical electronics projects and tutorials
- **Why watch:** Accessible project-based electronics education. Good for power supply design, PCB design, and general building skills.
- *(Complements: Part 0)*

**ElectroBOOM**
- **Focus:** Electronics + comedy (learning through controlled failure)
- **Why watch:** Entertaining and surprisingly educational. Mehdi's willingness to shock himself for science makes fundamental concepts memorable.
- *(Complements: Part 0)*

## 3. Forums and Online Communities

### General DIY Audio

**diyAudio.com**
- **What:** THE largest online community for DIY audio. Hundreds of thousands of members. Active since 2001.
- **Key sections:** Solid State, Tubes/Valves, Full Range, Multi-Way, Subwoofers, Class D, Digital, Headphone Systems, Group Buys
- **Why join:** This is where the deepest technical knowledge lives. If you have a question about speaker design, amplifier topology, or crossover optimization, someone on diyAudio has already answered it in detail. Search before posting — the archives are a goldmine.
- **Culture:** Technical, rigorous, occasionally blunt. New members should read extensively before posting.
- *(Relevant to: all Parts)*

### Guitar Pedals

**r/diypedals** (Reddit)
- **What:** Reddit's DIY pedal community (~120k members). Active, welcoming, build-focused.
- **Why join:** Great for showing builds, getting feedback, troubleshooting problems, and discovering new circuits. Weekly threads for beginners. More accessible and less intimidating than some dedicated forums.
- *(Relevant to: Part 1)*

**DIYStompboxes.com**
- **What:** One of the original guitar pedal building forums. Deep archives of circuit analysis and original designs.
- **Why join:** Historical depth. Many classic circuit analyses live here. Less active than its peak but still valuable for searching archives.
- *(Relevant to: Part 1)*

**FreeStompBoxes.org**
- **What:** Community focused on open-source guitar pedal designs. Extensive collection of verified schematics, PCB layouts, and build documentation.
- **Why join:** Free, community-verified pedal designs. Good source for your first builds and for learning circuit design by studying verified, working circuits.
- *(Relevant to: Part 1)*

**PedalPCB Forum**
- **What:** The community forum for PedalPCB (a major source of PCB designs for popular pedal circuits).
- **Why join:** Build support, troubleshooting, and discussion specific to PedalPCB's extensive catalog of pedal PCB designs.
- *(Relevant to: Part 1)*

### Speakers

**r/diysound** (Reddit)
- **What:** Reddit's DIY speaker community (~60k members).
- **Why join:** Build photos, design discussions, measurement data sharing. Good for getting feedback on your designs before building.
- *(Relevant to: Part 2)*

**Parts Express Tech Talk Forum**
- **What:** Parts Express's community forum. Focused on speaker building.
- **Why join:** Direct access to the Parts Express community and occasionally their engineering staff. Good discussions around Dayton Audio products specifically.
- *(Relevant to: Part 2)*

### Professional Audio / Recording

**GroupDIY**
- **What:** Community focused on building professional audio equipment — studio preamps, compressors, equalizers, mixing consoles.
- **Why join:** If you want to build pro audio gear (not just consumer and guitar), GroupDIY has deep expertise. Many members are professional audio engineers.
- *(Relevant to: Part 3, Part 6)*

### Amplifiers

**The Amp Garage**
- **What:** Forum dedicated to guitar and bass amplifier building and modification.
- **Why join:** Focused exclusively on amps. Good troubleshooting community and design discussions.
- *(Relevant to: Part 3)*

**r/diyaudio** (Reddit)
- **What:** Reddit's broader DIY audio community (~90k members). Covers amps, speakers, and everything in between.
- **Why join:** Active community with a mix of beginners and experts. Good for general questions that span multiple audio disciplines.
- *(Relevant to: all Parts)*

### Digital Audio

**ASR (Audio Science Review)**
- **What:** Measurement-focused audio community. Extensive database of equipment measurements (DACs, amps, headphones, speakers).
- **Why join:** If you want objective measurement data to inform your designs, ASR has the largest publicly available database. Their methodology is standardized, making comparisons meaningful.
- **Culture:** Strongly measurement-focused. Subjective opinions are less welcome. This is a feature if you want data; a bug if you want discussions about "how it sounds."
- *(Relevant to: Part 4)*

## 4. Free Online Resources

### Tube Amplifiers

**Rob Robinette's Website** — [robrobinette.com](https://robrobinette.com)
- **What:** The most comprehensive free resource on tube guitar amplifier modification and design. Covers every major guitar amp circuit (Fender, Marshall, Vox) with detailed modification guides, theory explanations, and safety information.
- **Why it's essential:** Rob explains complex tube amp concepts clearly and practically. His signal flow analysis, bias tutorial, and modification pages are reference-quality. Completely free.
- **Start with:** "How Tube Amps Work" and "Fender AB763 Signal Trace"
- *(Complements: Part 3)*

**Valve Wizard** — [valvewizard.co.uk](https://valvewizard.co.uk)
- **What:** Comprehensive free resource on tube preamp design by Merlin Blencowe (author of the preamp design books). Covers gain stage design, tone stacks, cathode followers, phase inverters, and power supply design with mathematical rigor.
- **Why it's essential:** If you want to design (not just build) tube preamp circuits, Valve Wizard teaches the theory from first principles. Each page includes worked examples and design equations.
- *(Complements: Part 3)*

### Solid-State Audio

**Elliott Sound Products (ESP)** — [sound.au](https://sound.au)
- **What:** Rod Elliott's massive library of audio circuit designs and technical articles. Hundreds of projects covering power amplifiers, preamplifiers, crossovers, power supplies, test equipment, and audio theory.
- **Why it's essential:** One of the oldest and most comprehensive DIY audio websites. Projects are practical, well-documented, and designed for real-world use. The technical articles on grounding, noise, and PCB layout are excellent.
- **Start with:** "Beginners' Guide to Electronics" and the Project Index
- *(Complements: Part 0, Part 3)*

### General Electronics

**All About Circuits** — [allaboutcircuits.com](https://allaboutcircuits.com)
- **What:** A complete, free online electronics textbook covering DC, AC, semiconductors, digital, and microcontrollers. Also includes worksheets, a community forum, and an EDA tool.
- **Why it's essential:** If you want a structured, textbook-style electronics education for free, this is it. Well-organized with progressive difficulty.
- *(Complements: Part 0)*

**Electronics Tutorials** — [electronics-tutorials.ws](https://electronics-tutorials.ws)
- **What:** Free tutorial site covering fundamental electronics topics with clear diagrams and examples.
- **Why it's useful:** Good for quick reference on specific topics (how does a Zener diode work? what's a Darlington pair?). Not as deep as All About Circuits but faster to navigate.
- *(Complements: Part 0)*

**TubeBooks.org** — [tubeBooks.org](https://tubebooks.org)
- **What:** Free archive of vintage electronics and audio technical books, including the original RCA Receiving Tube Manual, Radiotron Designer's Handbook, and many others.
- **Why it's essential:** Primary source material from the golden age of tube electronics. The RCA manual is the definitive tube reference. All public domain or with permission.
- *(Complements: Part 3)*

### PCB Design

**KiCad Official Documentation** — [docs.kicad.org](https://docs.kicad.org)
- **What:** The official KiCad documentation, covering all aspects of the software from schematic entry to PCB layout to manufacturing output.
- **Why it's essential:** The primary reference for KiCad. Updated with each release.
- *(Complements: Part 5)*

**Contextual Electronics** — YouTube channel by Chris Gammell
- **What:** KiCad tutorials and PCB design courses.
- **Why it's useful:** Practical, project-based approach to learning KiCad and PCB design.
- *(Complements: Part 5)*

## 5. Design Software

All of the following are free unless otherwise noted.

### Circuit Simulation

**LTspice** — [analog.com/en/design-center/design-tools-and-calculators/ltspice-simulator.html](https://analog.com/en/design-center/design-tools-and-calculators/ltspice-simulator.html)
- **What:** Free SPICE circuit simulator from Analog Devices.
- **Why it's essential:** The standard for analog circuit simulation. Design, test, and optimize your circuits before building them. Simulate amplifier gain, filter response, power supply ripple, and more.
- **Platform:** Windows, macOS (Wine for Linux)
- **Learning curve:** Moderate. Unintuitive UI but extremely powerful once learned.
- *(Complements: Part 0, Part 1, Part 3)*

**Falstad Circuit Simulator** — [falstad.com/circuit](https://falstad.com/circuit)
- **What:** Free, browser-based circuit simulator with animated current flow.
- **Why it's useful:** Instant visual feedback on how circuits work. Excellent for learning and quick experiments. No installation required.
- **Platform:** Any web browser
- **Learning curve:** Low. Drag-and-drop components.
- *(Complements: Part 0)*

**QUCS-S** — [ra3xdh.github.io](https://ra3xdh.github.io)
- **What:** Free, open-source SPICE simulation frontend. More modern UI than LTspice.
- **Platform:** Windows, macOS, Linux
- **Learning curve:** Moderate

### PCB Design

**KiCad** — [kicad.org](https://kicad.org)
- **What:** Free, open-source PCB design suite. Schematic editor, PCB layout, 3D viewer, and manufacturing output generation.
- **Why it's essential:** Industry-capable PCB design for free. Used by hobbyists and professionals. Extensive component libraries. Active community.
- **Platform:** Windows, macOS, Linux
- **Learning curve:** Moderate to steep. Worth the investment.
- *(Complements: Part 5)*

**EasyEDA** — [easyeda.com](https://easyeda.com)
- **What:** Free, browser-based PCB design tool, integrated with LCSC components and JLCPCB manufacturing.
- **Why it's useful:** Lower learning curve than KiCad. Components from LCSC are built into the library with real-time stock and pricing. Seamless export to JLCPCB for ordering.
- **Platform:** Web browser
- **Learning curve:** Low to moderate
- *(Complements: Part 5)*

### Speaker Enclosure Design

**WinISD** — [winisd.app](https://winisd.app) (or search for WinISD Pro Alpha)
- **What:** Free software for modeling speaker enclosures. Input Thiele-Small parameters, design sealed/ported/bandpass enclosures, and predict frequency response, group delay, port velocity, and cone excursion.
- **Why it's essential:** The standard tool for speaker enclosure design. Every DIY speaker designer uses it (or something based on the same math).
- **Platform:** Windows (runs under Wine on macOS/Linux)
- **Learning curve:** Low. Input T/S parameters, select enclosure type, adjust volume and tuning.
- *(Complements: Part 2)*

**VituixCAD** — [kimmmo.info](https://kimmmo.info)
- **What:** Free software for speaker crossover design and system optimization. Models frequency response, impedance, directivity, power response, and off-axis behavior.
- **Why it's essential:** More capable than most commercial crossover design tools. The ability to model off-axis behavior and power response sets it apart. Used by many serious DIY speaker designers.
- **Platform:** Windows (runs under Wine on macOS/Linux)
- **Learning curve:** Moderate to steep. Requires measured driver data.
- *(Complements: Part 2)*

### Room Measurement and DSP

**REW (Room EQ Wizard)** — [roomeqwizard.com](https://roomeqwizard.com)
- **What:** Free software for acoustic measurement and analysis. Measures frequency response, RT60, waterfall, impedance, THD, and more.
- **Why it's essential:** The standard for DIY acoustic measurement. Pairs with a calibrated USB microphone (miniDSP UMIK-1). Measures speakers, rooms, amplifiers, and complete systems.
- **Platform:** Windows, macOS, Linux
- **Learning curve:** Moderate. Many features, but basic speaker measurement is straightforward.
- *(Complements: Part 2, Part 6)*

**CamillaDSP** — [github.com/HEnquist/camilladsp](https://github.com/HEnquist/camilladsp)
- **What:** Free, open-source DSP engine for audio processing. Runs on a Raspberry Pi or any Linux/macOS/Windows computer. Supports FIR and IIR filters, crossovers, equalization, and room correction.
- **Why it's essential:** The most capable free DSP platform for audio. Use it for active crossovers, room correction, speaker equalization, and multi-channel processing.
- **Platform:** Linux (including Raspberry Pi), macOS, Windows
- **Learning curve:** Moderate to steep. Configuration via YAML files.
- *(Complements: Part 4, Part 6)*

### CAD for Mechanical Design

**Fusion 360** — [autodesk.com/products/fusion-360](https://autodesk.com/products/fusion-360)
- **What:** Free (for personal/hobby use) 3D CAD software. Parametric modeling, assembly design, and CAM (CNC toolpath generation).
- **Why it's useful:** Design speaker cabinets, amplifier chassis, custom enclosures, and jigs. Export for CNC cutting or 3D printing.
- **Platform:** Windows, macOS
- **Learning curve:** Moderate

**FreeCAD** — [freecad.org](https://freecad.org)
- **What:** Free, open-source 3D CAD software.
- **Why it's useful:** Alternative to Fusion 360 without the cloud dependency. Steeper learning curve but fully capable.
- **Platform:** Windows, macOS, Linux

## 7. Kit Suppliers

Building from a kit is the best way to learn, because someone else has already verified the design, selected the components, and written the instructions. Here are the best sources.

### Guitar Pedal Kits

**Aion FX**
- **Website:** [aionfx.com](https://aionfx.com)
- **What:** High-quality PCBs and kits for classic and original pedal circuits. Excellent documentation with build guides, schematics, and mod suggestions.
- **Why recommended:** The documentation quality is outstanding. Each build document includes the schematic, a component explanation, and suggested modifications. Great for learning.

**PedalPCB**
- **Website:** [pedalpcb.com](https://pedalpcb.com)
- **What:** The largest catalog of PCB designs for popular pedal circuits. Sells PCBs (you source components) and some kits.
- **Why recommended:** Huge selection covering virtually every popular pedal circuit. Community forum for build support.

**BYOC (Build Your Own Clone)**
- **Website:** [buildyourownclone.com](https://buildyourownclone.com)
- **What:** Complete pedal kits including all components, enclosure, knobs, and detailed instructions.
- **Why recommended:** The most beginner-friendly pedal kits. Everything is included, and the instructions are step-by-step with photos.

**StewMac (Stewart-MacDonald)**
- **Website:** [stewmac.com](https://stewmac.com)
- **What:** Guitar building and pedal kits. Their pedal kits are simple and well-documented.
- **Why recommended:** Good for absolute beginners. Higher price but excellent instructions.

### Amplifier Kits

**Bottlehead**
- **Website:** [bottlehead.com](https://bottlehead.com)
- **What:** Hi-fi tube amplifier, preamp, and headphone amp kits. The Crack headphone amp is legendary.
- **Why recommended:** Exceptional kit quality and documentation. The Crack headphone amplifier kit (~$300) is widely considered the best introduction to tube amplifier building. Active community forum.

**Tube Depot**
- **Website:** [tubedepot.com](https://tubedepot.com)
- **What:** Guitar amplifier kits — Fender Tweed Deluxe, Marshall 18W, Vox AC15 clones.
- **Why recommended:** Complete kits with everything included. Good instructions. One of the most popular starting points for tube guitar amp building.

**Mojotone**
- **Website:** [mojotone.com](https://mojotone.com)
- **What:** Guitar amplifier kits with their own transformers and components.
- **Why recommended:** High-quality components. Kits range from simple single-ended amps to complex multi-channel designs.

**TubeCAD / GlassWare**
- **Website:** [glass-ware.com](https://glass-ware.com)
- **What:** PCBs and kits for John Broskie's original tube circuit designs. Unique topologies (Aikido, SRPP variants) not found elsewhere.
- **Why recommended:** For builders who want something beyond standard Fender/Marshall clones.

### Speaker Kits

**Parts Express**
- **Website:** [partsexpress.com](https://partsexpress.com)
- **What:** Complete speaker kits (drivers, crossover components, port tubes) and flat-pack cabinet kits.
- **Why recommended:** The broadest selection of speaker kits at various price points. Flat-pack cabinets save significant time — pre-cut MDF panels that you glue together.

**Meniscus Audio**
- **Website:** [meniscusaudio.com](https://meniscusaudio.com)
- **What:** Speaker kits with premium crossover components. Curated designs.
- **Why recommended:** Higher-end kits than Parts Express, using premium crossover parts.

**CSS Audio (Creative Sound Solutions)**
- **Website:** [cssaudio.com](https://cssaudio.com)
- **What:** Custom speaker drivers and complete kits. Known for their SDX15 subwoofer driver and the Criton line of speaker kits.
- **Why recommended:** Drivers are designed by audio engineer Danny Richie, with published measurements.

### Digital Audio / DSP Kits

**miniDSP**
- **Website:** [minidsp.com](https://minidsp.com)
- **What:** DSP boards (2x4 HD, SHD series) for active crossovers, room correction, and multi-channel audio processing.
- **Why recommended:** Plug-and-play DSP for audio builders. The miniDSP 2x4 HD is the standard for DIY active crossover projects.

**HiFiBerry**
- **Website:** [hifiberry.com](https://hifiberry.com)
- **What:** High-quality DAC and amplifier HATs (Hardware Attached on Top) for Raspberry Pi.
- **Why recommended:** Easily add high-quality audio output to Raspberry Pi projects. The DAC2 Pro and Amp2 are popular choices for streamer and active speaker builds.

## 8. Quick Reference: Where to Start by Interest

If you know what you want to build but aren't sure where to begin learning, start here:

### "I want to build guitar pedals"
1. Read: *Electronic Projects for Musicians* (Anderton) or *How to Modify Guitar Pedals* (Wampler)
2. Watch: JHS Pedals, DIY Guitar Pedals
3. Join: r/diypedals, FreeStompBoxes.org
4. Build: A BYOC or PedalPCB kit
5. Design tool: LTspice, then KiCad

### "I want to build speakers"
1. Read: *Loudspeaker Design Cookbook* (Dickason)
2. Watch: Toid's DIY Audio
3. Join: diyAudio.com, r/diysound
4. Build: A Parts Express flat-pack kit
5. Design tools: WinISD, VituixCAD, REW + UMIK-1

### "I want to build tube amplifiers"
1. Read: *Design and Construction of Tube Guitar Amplifiers* (Megantz)
2. Watch: Uncle Doug
3. Browse: Rob Robinette's website, Valve Wizard
4. Join: diyAudio.com (Tubes/Valves section), The Amp Garage
5. Build: A Tube Depot or Mojotone amp kit
6. Design tools: LTspice (with tube SPICE models)

### "I want to build solid-state / chip amplifiers"
1. Read: *Audio Power Amplifier Design* (Douglas Self)
2. Browse: Elliott Sound Products (sound.au)
